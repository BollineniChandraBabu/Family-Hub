What Happened

That Thursday, Chandu and Sindhu had come from Hyderabad together. The journey was ordinary — nothing in it announced what the day was about to become. When they arrived, her parents were there. Nobody had told him they were coming. They sat down to talk.

The conversation turned to the things that had been accumulating between them — problems that had grown over time, wounds that had not healed, words spoken and unspoken, the weight of a marriage that had been struggling under pressures neither had fully been able to resolve. There was no single reason. There never is.

At some point, the conversation became a decision. Sindhu chose to leave. She walked out with her family, and the home they had shared became, in that single moment, just a place. Chandu stood in the silence that followed — the kind of silence that means something permanent has just occurred.

06
Words Never Sent
To Sindhu

There are things about that day I keep returning to. Not the argument itself, but the surprise of it — the fact that I didn't know your family was coming. The whole shape of the day arrived without any warning, and I didn't have time to prepare for it in any way. That isn't a complaint. It's just an honest accounting of what happened.

I understand, now more than I did then, that the problems between us had been building for a long time. That no single conversation was going to fix them. That you had reached a point where leaving felt like the only real answer available to you. I don't know that I could have changed it, even if I had known it was coming. But I wish we had found a way to have that conversation differently, at some point, before it became the last one.

You were carrying Yokshith when you left. He arrived on September 2nd, 2025 — already loved, from long before that day. Whatever happened between us, that has always been true and will always remain so.

I keep this page not to place blame or to grieve publicly. I keep it because April 3rd happened, it was real, and real things deserve to be acknowledged honestly — even when the honest version is complicated and doesn't resolve into anything clean.

Written with honesty, kept with care —
Chandu
